Why now

Look at them,
Em.

Your “when Ellie goes to college” line makes me laugh every time. And the plan to save the Subaru for her: I love how far ahead you think. But thirteen years from now, that car isn’t what either of us wants for our daughter.

And by then, this version of our three is gone too. They’ll be different kids.

I want the truck while it’s still these three. While Cole still races Ellie for shotgun. While Luke is still small enough to throw in a car seat.

When Ellie turns sixteen, the Subaru will be 15 years old with our miles on it, two safety generations behind. By then we’ll be in a position to give her something newer, safer. An SUV that fits her life and tells her, we wanted her to have it.

I want this in their memories of us, Em. And in hers.

The boy

He wanted one since he was eight.

He drew big trucks in the margins of every notebook. Sat in his dad’s truck and pretended to drive. Told anyone who would listen that he’d drive his own one day.

Then he grew up. Joined the Guard. Built a career. Married a beautiful girl named Emily. Had three amazing kids. Deployed. Paid off debt. Kept saying not yet.

But through all of that, he is still that little boy.

For twenty years, the truck has been my measuring stick. Am I close enough yet? Am I there? Can I do this yet?

And now, because of what we’ve built, I finally am.

Our kids will get older, our lives will get more complicated, there will always be a reason why it’s not the right time. But the time is now to make a little boy’s dreams come true.
